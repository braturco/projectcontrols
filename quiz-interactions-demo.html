Pattern 02 — Clickable Contract Document

An exercise activates click targets on specific contract clauses. The document looks normal until exercise mode is toggled — then hotspots light up.

RIDGELINE_PIPELINE_CONTRACT_v3.docx ⬡ exercise mode
Exercise — Select the correct clause
Which clause establishes the contractor's obligation for environmental compliance?

1. Scope of Work

The Contractor shall perform all geotechnical drilling, soil sampling, and environmental site assessment activities as described in Schedule A, inclusive of all Borehole Program locations identified in the Ridgeline Pipeline Environmental Assessment.

2. Project Schedule

All field work activities shall be completed no later than September 15 of the current field season. The Contractor acknowledges that migratory bird nesting restrictions under the Migratory Birds Convention Act apply between April 1 and July 31, and shall schedule clearing and vegetation disturbance accordingly.

3. Environmental Compliance

The Contractor shall at all times conduct operations in accordance with applicable federal and provincial environmental legislation, including the Alberta Environmental Protection and Enhancement Act, and all conditions of the AER authorizations issued for this Project. Spill response plans shall be maintained on-site at all times.

4. Reporting & Documentation

Field data including borehole logs, chain-of-custody forms, and photographic documentation shall be submitted to the Project Manager within five (5) business days of each mobilization completing field activities. Deliverables shall conform to the formats specified in Schedule B.

5. Payment Terms

Invoices shall be submitted monthly against approved progress, referencing the applicable WBS codes. The Owner's Representative shall have fourteen (14) days to review and approve invoices prior to payment. Disputed amounts shall not delay payment of undisputed balances.

▸ Clickable zones are invisible until "Start Exercise" is triggered. Only correct targets advance the exercise.
